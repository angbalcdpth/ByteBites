REFLECTION
--

Students should understand how to leverage an AI agent in order to translate a UML design into working model classes that match the specs required as well as how to apply individual knowledge and test the results/revisions given by the agent.

Students are most likely to struggle with ambiguous design choices, finding the balance of how much of AI suggestion to use, and how to tailor the agent to best execute their plan.  

AI was most helpful for fast scaffolding, reducing repetetive tasks, expanding on given foundations, providing pytest structures, and generating good behavior scenarios to test. It was most misleading when creating extra features not required, missed some suggestions from user occasionally,  and when suggested code drifted from required method and attribute contracts.  

One way to guide a student without giving away the answer is to ask them to think first of what they believe should be included in the UML design and if the AI helped them make it clear and detailed or did it add confusion and unnecessary fluff. Based on their response I would then ask them how they can better tailor their prompt to avoid the latter.
